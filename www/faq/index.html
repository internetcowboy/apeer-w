FAQ

What is Apeer?

Apeer is a mobile app that gives attorneys the power to assign their court appearances to other experienced attorneys (“Peers”).

Is Apeer available in my city?

Apeer is currently available in Los Angeles and will soon be released in San Francisco and other US cities. Add your email address here to be notified when Apeer arrives in your area.

Is Apeer a law firm?

No. Apeer is a platform that connects attorneys and empowers them with innovative technology to exchange, manage, and streamline court appearances. Apeer also handles the administrative and transactional work - like document management and payment processing - so attorneys can focus on being attorneys.

How do I sign up?

Apeer is available via the Apple App Store and Google Play. Simply search “Apeer for Attorneys” and download the app. Registration is quick, easy and free.

What do you mean by “Principal” and “Peer”?

We refer to an attorney of record as a “Principal” and their appearance attorney as a “Peer”.

Are Peers vetted?

Yes. Attorneys must meet certain standards to accept appearances through Apeer. These vetting requirements include an unblemished State Bar record in good and active standing and more than one year of solid court experience in their respective practice areas. The truth is that our Peers have been practicing excellent law for a long time. Our review and feedback process promotes this ongoing commitment to performance. Accountability is at the heart of our work.

How does Apeer work?

1. ASSIGN
To request coverage a Principal taps “Assign” and submits the requested appearance details. Once the Principal reviews and approves the appearance cost, Apeer notifies qualified Peers and provides them the information necessary to perform a conflicts check (e.g. the identities of the client and opposing parties). As soon as a Peer accepts the request the Principal is notified and the attorneys are connected.

2. CONNECT
The Principal is responsible for providing the information and documentation necessary for the Peer to successfully complete the appearance. Likewise, the Peer is responsible to prepare, present, and handle the matter professionally. Apeer provides all of the functionality you would expect - like chat and file sharing - in order to collaborate and complete the matter.

3. REVIEW
The Peer submits a summary report and any related documentation the day of the appearance. Thereafter, the Principal’s payment card is charged and the appearance is archived for future reference. Each attorney is asked to rate the other to ensure the highest quality of service across Apeer.

Can I also make appearances?

Simply tap the “Become a Peer” button in your profile to apply. You will receive an email to assist you through the vetting process.

Can I assign an appearance by phone or web?

Assigning appearances is done exclusively through the app on your iPhone or Android. We plan to add browser functionality so that you can add and manage appearances on your desktop, laptop, or tablet.

Can I assign an appearance on the same day as the hearing?

You can submit your request anytime up until shortly before the hearing. However, the closer to the time of the hearing your request is made the less likely a Peer will be available to accept your request and be able to get to court in time, with the necessary information.

Can I request a specific attorney?

Not at this time. Apeer is designed to get your appearances covered fast with the power of talent at the tips of your fingers.

We are working on a future feature that will allow you to add colleagues to a private network and choose who may accept your request.

What types of hearings can I assign through Apeer?

You can currently assign basic, non-contested hearings such as an arraignment, case management conference, etc. More complex appearances will be available soon.

How much does an appearance cost?

You are only charged once an appearance is complete and you will always be asked to review the cost before you commit to anything. The appearance cost consists of:

$75 Appearance Fee (remitted to the Peer)
$14 Service Charge


Courthouse parking fees may increase the Appearance Fee (by no more than $15).

How is payment handled?

Before assigning your first appearance you will be asked to add a credit or debit card. This card will be saved securely for your future assignments. You can update your payment card under your profile tab. You will only be charged when your Peer completes the appearance.

What if I wish to cancel an assigned appearance?

You can cancel your assignment at any time. There is no fee to cancel before a Peer accepts your request.

Cancellation fee after a Peer has been assigned:
Before 48 hours of hearing: $25
Within 48 hours of hearing: 50% of appearance cost
Within 24 hours of hearing: 100% of appearance cost

What should I do after a Peer has been assigned my appearance?

Once you receive a notification that a Peer has been assigned you can chat with them and share files in-app. It is up to you to ensure they have all instructions, information, and documentation necessary to successfully and competently handle the appearance.

How do I communicate with my Peer?

Apeer’s chat feature delivers real-time communication between attorneys.

Can I share documents with my Peer?

Yes. Snap and send a photo of notes or information you want to share on the fly. Attach any file directly from your Dropbox (Android users can access additional sources). Other documents can be emailed to your Peer.

Can my Peer accept service of discovery or other documentation and deliver the originals to me?

Yes. To receive original documents simply provide your Peer with the request and the appropriate mailing address.

When do I receive an appearance report?

Your Peer will submit a summary report along with any new documentation on the day of the appearance. A push notification will alert you as soon as the materials are available in the app. You will also receive the summary report by email with any corresponding documents attached.

Is my Peer accountable?

For the scope of the assigned appearance, your Peer is subject to the same rules of professional conduct that apply to any attorney-client relationship. Only Peers with unblemished State Bar records are allowed to accept appearances through Apeer. Peers are required to meet all applicable ethical and other duties, including the duty of confidentiality and the standard of care.

What if an emergency prevents my Peer from making the appearance?

Peers are instructed to notify Apeer Support in an emergency. We will work diligently to replace them immediately. In the event that it is not possible to replace them, the Peer is responsible for any sanctions associated with their failure to appear and is subject to removal from the Apeer platform.

What if there is an issue with my appearance?

If there is a problem with the appearance you should notify Apeer Support and we will investigate your concerns. Responsibility for the appearance rests solely between you and your Peer, however, we will do whatever we can to ensure an exceptional experience.

Are there Peer (and Principal) reviews?

Yes. After an appearance is completed each attorney rates the other by tapping one of three options: “poor”, “good”, or “great”. There is also the opportunity to include additional feedback. Submitting a “poor” rating effectively initiates our dispute resolution process. Apeer Support will investigate the matter to determine what action, if any, is necessary to address concerns. An attorney who receives a rating of less than “good” is subject to removal from Apeer.

Can I access records from a past appearance?

Records (e.g. chat history, files, and summary report) are archived in Case History and available exclusively to the Principal for one year after the appearance.
